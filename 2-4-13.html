WCAG 2.4.13: Focus Appearance

View main list

Brief summary: The focus indicator must have sufficient size and contrast so users can identify the focused component.

View the W3C Understanding page for WCAG 2.4.13 Focus Appearance.

Observed SiteLint behavior

SiteLint uses focus-appearance as a manual-verification trigger for WCAG 2.4.13. It does not appear to measure focus-indicator size, thickness, or contrast on the examples.

The example link points to a real page so unrelated same-page-link findings do not obscure focus-appearance behavior.

Recommendation

Keep focus-appearance as a manual prompt unless SiteLint can inspect rendered focus states. Improved coverage should measure the focus indicator's area and thickness and compare its contrast against adjacent colors.

Passing examples

Passing example 1: Focus indicator is thick and high contrast

The focus outline has visible thickness, offset, and contrast against the page background.

Failing examples

Failing example 1: Focus indicator is too thin

The one-pixel indicator is unlikely to meet the focus appearance area requirement.

Failing example 2: Focus indicator has low contrast

The indicator is present, but the contrast against the adjacent background is weak.

Failing example 3: Focus indicator is a subtle color change only

A slight background shift may not provide enough area or contrast to identify focus.
